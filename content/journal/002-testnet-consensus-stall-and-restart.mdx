July 21 to 23 UTC on our testnet node. Slot numbers, hashes, and log fragments are
from the box.

## What happened?

On July 21 testnet consensus stalled. As described in the public restart
instructions, the root cause was a bug in the jito-solana / BAM client: strict
nonce size checks enabled on a consensus codepath that stock Agave did not have
enabled. Nodes running jito wedged and stopped making progress; the cluster
stopped finalizing.

Our node was running jito-solana 4.2.0-beta.2. By ~04:00 UTC on July 22 it was
wedged: `us:` frozen at **423534755**, gaining 0.0 slots/sec, ~35k slots behind.
Cluster guidance was clear: jito/BAM operators move to Agave.

What follows is the recovery, step by step.

## Switching clients mid-incident

07:19 to 07:48 UTC: I built stock Agave v4.2.0-beta.2 from source. The binary
swap was not drop-in. The stock validator rejects jito-only flags, so the start
script needed five of them stripped:

```text
--tip-payment-program-pubkey
--tip-distribution-program-pubkey
--merkle-root-upload-authority
--block-engine-url
--shred-receiver-address
```

I wrote an Agave-only start script, repointed systemd at it, and first booted
with `--no-voting`. That was not a generic “get healthy first” habit. Voting into
an unfinalized, stalled cluster risks lockout on a dead fork, and in the worst
case a slashable vote. Until the cluster had a coordinated path out, the node
should run and catch up without joining vote consensus.

## The wedged ledger

The node came up on Agave at 07:48, still pinned at the wedge slot. The on-disk
RocksDB ledger was stuck at the pre-stall state, and the new client happily
resumed from it. I moved the ledger aside; the node rebuilt from snapshot and
began replaying forward at ~5 to 6 slots/sec.

Replay advanced. Rooting did not. `highest-super-majority-root` sat frozen at
**423534728** for over five hours. That number being stuck was not a local
problem: nothing cluster-wide was finalizing, which also meant no fresh snapshots
existed anywhere, since snapshots are generated at rooted slots. You cannot
snapshot-hop your way out of a cluster that has stopped rooting.

Around 11:47 the node hit the same `solReplayTx` stack overflow crash other
operators reported. `Restart=always` caught it. Not memory pressure: 125 GB box,
~34 GB used.

## The coordinated restart

~19:00 UTC: coordinated restart organized, restart slot **423585904** agreed
cluster-wide. I stopped the node and verified the blockstore before touching
anything:

```bash
agave-ledger-tool blockstore latest-optimistic-slots
```

Our latest optimistic slots matched the cluster's agreed restart point exactly.

Then I generated the hard-fork restart snapshot (~18 minutes of replay),
producing `incremental-snapshot-423525841-423585904-6veTfzos….tar.zst`.

**The hash trap:** the restart instructions state bank hash `9x8k…`. The
generated snapshot file hashes as `6veT…`. I read that mismatch as a bad snapshot
and regenerated before working out why the two differ. They are different
objects: the post-hard-fork **startup bank hash** (what `--expected-bank-hash`
wants) versus the **snapshot file hash** (content identity of the tarball). The
instructions were correct. Comparing them as if they should match burns time at
the worst moment.

19:34: restarted with the hard-fork trio:

```text
--wait-for-supermajority 423585904
--expected-shred-version 27960
--expected-bank-hash 9x8k…
```

That is also when voting became safe again. `--wait-for-supermajority` is the
protection that replaced the earlier `--no-voting` stance: the node holds off
until enough stake is at the restart slot, then joins consensus cleanly.

One thing Agave got exactly right: it detected the hard fork, **discarded the
stale tower automatically**, and rebuilt it from the vote account. No manual
tower deletion, no double-vote risk, no intervention. That is the anxiety point
on a first hard-fork restart, and the client handled it without a hand edit.

## The wait

Then thirteen and a half hours of watching stake percentages climb in gossip:
45.7% → 47.8% → 49.6% → 68% → 74% → 80% threshold. From 19:45 on July 22 to
~09:20 on July 23. There is nothing to do in this phase except not touch the
node. At ~09:20 the cluster crossed 80%, the node resumed, and
Processed/Confirmed/Finalized advanced in step. Recovered, though the cluster
kept reconverging for hours after.

## What was lost

Honesty first. Epoch 993 vote credits landed at ~60% versus the 98 to 99% of prior
epochs, and block production for the stall window is gone. Epoch 993 skip rate
**36.4%**. That is what a cluster-wide stall costs even when the recovery goes
cleanly: the node did nothing wrong after the switch, and the epoch numbers still
carry the scar.

## After recovery

The incident did not end the moment slots advanced again. The morning after,
while the cluster was still reconverging: **11 missed votes**, with the node
stable throughout (**NRestarts 0**) and fork majority share at **77.8%**,
consistent with votes landing on forks that lost, not with a local fault.
Finality lag stayed elevated near **~44 slots** while stake reconverged. Easy to
read as “your validator is broken.” It was not. Verify before you “fix” a
healthy box.

## The one still open

Logrotate orphaned the validator log at midnight. The postrotate `USR1` signal is
configured correctly, yet the validator kept writing to the rotated file
descriptor. Unresolved for now: not the stall, not the restart; separate issue,
still open.

## What I learned

- **`--no-voting` and `--wait-for-supermajority` solve different moments.** Mid
  stall, do not vote into an unfinalized cluster. On a coordinated restart, the
  supermajority wait is what makes voting safe again.
- **Name the artifact before you compare hashes.** Startup bank hash and snapshot
  file hash are not supposed to match. I burned a regenerate on that mistake;
  the restart instructions were right.
